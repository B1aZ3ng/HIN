Common Pool Resources (CPR) are resources that are [[rivalrous]] and non-[[excludable]], meaning that there is only a limited amount, but you cant exclude other people from using it. 

The [[Tragedy of the Commons]] states that individuals/firms acting in their own self-interest will deplete common pool resources. This is because the fact that its rivalrous means that there is scarcity which is the premise needed for value. This means that there is a high private benefit but low private cose (MPB > MPC), meaning the individuals/firms would increase production using this resource.

However there is a [[Externalities#Negative Production Externality|Negative Production Externality]] that results from this, meaning that there is an overallocation of resources. And it results in the resource getting more and more scarce, resulting in long term destruction

### Examples
Fish in the ocean
Clean Air
Forests
(clean) Water
...

## Tradeable Permits
Governments can implement tradeable permits by putting a market price on them through the distribution of tradeable "permits" which each allow firms to utilise a certain amount of CPR. allows firms to trade the distributed permits at market prices set by supply and demand

#### IRL
European Union Emissions Trading Scheme (EU ETS) 
- At the start, EU gave out too many, causing permit prices to be too low, meaning that it gave firms little incentive to reduce emissions due to the fact that they were willing and able to buy the permits.
- As a result, emissions did not fall by much
- After some reforms, and introduction of a Market Stability Reserve, permit price rose, encouraging firms to lower emissions by shifting to cleaner energy

#### Eval
- Short term - large impact on firms, due to how they were less willing and able to have emissions
- long term - as firms see more efficient solutions, lower emissions compared to same amount of the g/s = less permits needed
- depends on - the ability of governments to set an apt number of permits - too little greatly hrams firms, too much means no change

## International agreements

 ﻿﻿For global common access resources such as the ozone layer or climate stability, international cooperation is essential. Agreements allow countries to coordinate their actions and prevent free-riding, where one nation benefits from others reducing pollution while continuing to pollute itself. 
#### IRL﻿﻿
 ﻿﻿A powerful example is the Montreal Protocol, which successfully phased out ozone-depleting substances like CFCs through binding commitments from almost every country in the world. As a result, the ozone layer has begun to recover, making this one of the most successful examples of managing a global common access resource.
### EVAL﻿﻿
- ﻿﻿EVALUATION → DEPENDS ON... However, the effectiveness of global agreements depends on whether major emitters participate. The Paris Climate Agreement has struggled because some large countries such as the United States have withdrawn, and some others have failed to meet their voluntary targets, reducing the overall impact on emissions.
- ﻿﻿EVALUATION → DEPENDS ON... The effectiveness depends on the risk of free-riding.  
    This is where individual countries may choose to not join agreements, and prefer to let others bear the cost of reducing pollution while they continue to benefit from economic growth driven by fossil fuels. This weakens global efforts to protect the resource.
