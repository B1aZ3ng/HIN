
# Solid State Drive (SSD)
- Zap data electronically through NAND flash memory
	- stores charge in floating gate transistor.
	- To read, a charge is applied to transistor. to write a voltage is applied blocking flow for a 0 allowing flow of electrons through transistory
- No moving parts
- Pricier and smaller
- compact
- less heat
- fast
- silent
- choice for modern laptops and PCs
- non-volatile
# Hard Disk Drive (HDD)
Spins magnetic platters with a moving arm to read and write data
- cheaper large storage capacity
- slower, louder and more fragile
- rapidly spinning magnetic plate. 
- reads data through the magnetisation of tiny spots on the plate
- choice for servers
- non-volatile

# Optical Storage
using a laser beam to store and retrieve data
A Laser beam encodes digital data on an optical disc or laser disc in the form of tiny pits/grooves which will not reflect the laser back hence how it reads 1 or 0.
